How Our Safety Scoring Works

Safe Maternity uses a comprehensive, evidence-based approach to evaluate the safety of foods, medications, and activities during pregnancy. Our algorithm combines multiple data sources and medical research to provide you with reliable safety assessments.

The Safety Scale (1-10)

1-3
Safe
4-6
Caution
7-10
Avoid
Key Principle: When medical evidence conflicts, we always err on the side of caution to protect you and your baby. A higher safety score means more potential risk.

Our Algorithm Process

Every safety assessment follows a rigorous multi-step evaluation process:

1
Query Analysis
We identify the specific item, medication, or activity you're asking about, including brand names, ingredients, and contexts.
2
Database Lookup
We search our comprehensive database of over 10,000 items with pre-calculated safety scores based on medical literature.
3
AI Analysis
For items not in our database, our AI analyzes current medical research, FDA guidelines, and clinical studies in real-time.
4
Risk Factor Evaluation
We assess multiple risk factors including trimester-specific concerns, dosage considerations, and individual health conditions.
5
Safety Score Generation
A final score from 1-10 is calculated, with clear explanations and practical recommendations provided.

Special Considerations in Our Algorithm

Trimester-Specific Assessments

Safety can vary throughout pregnancy. Our algorithm considers:

  • First Trimester (Weeks 1-13): Critical organ development period with highest sensitivity
  • Second Trimester (Weeks 14-27): Generally safer period with continued growth
  • Third Trimester (Weeks 28-40): Focus on labor preparation and delivery concerns

Individual Risk Factors

Premium users can input personal health information for customized assessments based on:

  • Pre-existing medical conditions
  • Previous pregnancy complications
  • Multiple pregnancy (twins, triplets)
  • Age-related risk factors
  • Medication interactions

Dosage and Frequency

Many substances are safe in moderation but risky in excess. We evaluate:

  • Recommended daily limits
  • Cumulative exposure effects
  • Timing and duration of exposure

Quality Assurance

üìÖ Daily Updates: Our database is updated daily with the latest medical research and FDA announcements to ensure you always have access to current information.

Medical Review Process

All safety scores and recommendations undergo:

  1. Initial AI analysis based on medical literature
  2. Cross-referencing with multiple authoritative sources
  3. Regular audits by healthcare professionals
  4. Continuous feedback integration from user reports
  5. Quarterly comprehensive database reviews

Transparency Commitment

For every safety assessment, we provide:

  • Clear risk score with visual indicators
  • Detailed explanation of why something received its score
  • Specific trimester considerations when applicable
  • Practical alternatives for high-risk items
  • Links to primary sources and medical studies
